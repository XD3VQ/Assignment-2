Dendrochronology

(dendro - greek: "tree"; chrono(s) - greek: "time")

  • Rings are formed inside trees, each grown at the edge from the bark. As a tree grows outwards in size, so does its rings at a periodic rate,
    giving the tree a multi-ringed appearance when looked at vertically through.
  • One ring represents around one year of growth.
    • This is not guaranteed, as some rings develop too faintly to see and many other natural discrepancies can occur.
    • The width/size of the ring gradually decreases over the years, at a exponential decay rate.
